# Gross income tests (200% screening table vs 130%)

**Source id:** `nc-fns-gross-income-tests`
**Public source:** More In My Basket – Am I Eligible?
**URL:** https://morefood.org/using-snap/am-i-eligible/

## Two public ideas, one decision-maker

Public NC outreach materials often show a table labeled **Maximum Gross Monthly Income (200%)** — roughly **200% of the federal poverty level**. That table is what **this informal screening tool uses** for its simple before-tax income comparison (see also `nc-fns-income-limits` / the versioned ruleset in code).

The **same public materials** note that **some individuals or households must meet standard (stricter) gross income limits**, often described as about **130% of the federal poverty level**. **Whether that lower test applies is not decided by this tool.** A county Department of Social Services (DSS) eligibility worker applies the official rules for your situation.

## What that means on this screen

| Your informal screen result (200% table) | What it does *not* mean |
| ---------------------------------------- | ------------------------ |
| At or under the public 200% limit | You are approved. You might still face a **stricter (~130%)** gross test if DSS applies it. |
| Over the public 200% limit | You are unlikely to pass the usual gross screen. A stricter 130% test would also be harder to meet. |

In both cases: **only DSS can make an official determination.** This tool does **not**:

- Choose which gross test (200% vs 130%) applies to you
- Compute or publish a full 130% dollar table
- Replace a full SNAP eligibility review (resources, deductions, household composition, etc.)

## If you are asking “which limit do I use?”

- **For this chat tool:** the limit shown in your screening summary is from the **public 200% gross table** used by the POC ruleset.
- **For a real application:** use **NC ePASS** or your **county DSS** — they apply the correct test and all other rules.

# NC Food and Nutrition Services (FNS) overview

**Source id:** `nc-fns-overview`
**Public source:** NCDHHS Food and Nutrition Services
**URL:** https://www.ncdhhs.gov/divisions/child-and-family-well-being/food-and-nutrition-services-food-stamps

North Carolina’s Food and Nutrition Services (FNS) program is the state’s SNAP (Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program) / food-assistance program. It helps low-income individuals and families buy food.

## High-level factors that affect eligibility

Public materials state that FNS eligibility is determined by factors including:

- Income
- Household size
- Assets / resources (not fully modeled in this screening POC)
- Residency and other program rules
- Additional rules for students, work requirements (ABAWD), and non-citizen status (not fully modeled here)

## Who may be able to get benefits (outreach framing)

Public outreach materials often say people may be able to get benefits if they:

- Have no job or a low-paying job
- Receive certain other benefits (for example SSI or TANF/Work First)
- Are elderly or disabled and low-income
- Are experiencing homelessness

**This agent performs an informal likelihood screen only.** Only a county Department of Social Services (DSS) eligibility worker can make an official determination.

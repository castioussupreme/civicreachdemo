# College student SNAP/FNS rules (summary)

**Source id:** `nc-fns-student-rules`
**Public sources:**
- https://morefood.org/using-snap/am-i-eligible/
- https://www.fns.usda.gov/snap/students
- https://nccollegefoodbenefits.org/eligibility/

## Why students are special

College students enrolled at least half-time often face **additional eligibility requirements** beyond income and household size. Public materials describe these as **exemptions**—students typically must meet income rules **and** at least one student exemption.

## Example exemptions (illustrative, not exhaustive)

Outreach materials commonly list exemptions such as:

- Working 20+ hours per week in paid employment
- Participating in a state or federally financed work-study program
- Participating in an on-the-job training program
- Caring for a young dependent child (age thresholds vary by rule)
- Receiving TANF
- Age under 18 or over certain cutoffs (materials vary; DSS applies official rules)
- Disability that prevents work

## POC behavior

If the user indicates they are a college student, this agent:

1. Still may run a **gross income screen** when other facts are known.
2. Will **not** fully determine student eligibility.
3. Will report **uncertainty** and recommend applying / speaking with DSS or campus outreach for student-specific rules.
4. Will cite student policy sources.

**International students** are described in outreach materials as not eligible under current guidelines.
